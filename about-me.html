About Us!




Hi! My name is Chandana Akasapu and I am a rising junior in high school. I was motivated to creating a site about education inequities for girls in developing countries because I know a lot of girls in my parents’ home in South Asia, specifically India. These girls are exceedingly smart but their families don’t have the money to send them, or choose to marry them off at a young age, and there are a multitude of other situational reasons why these girls don’t get the education they deserve. In America, access to education is a human right and I believe that should be the standard everywhere, regardless of the GDP. I wouldn’t be able to achieve goals and strive for perfection in my life if it weren’t for the human rights of education given to me as an American citizen.

Hello! My name is Shreeya Prabu and I’m an eleventh grader from Irving, TX. I think that education should be a universal right and that everyone should be able to advance in society with the tool of knowledge. In many developing countries, females are not given the same educational opportunities as males and are instead left to work at the household, bearing children, cooking and cleaning. This continues the cycle of poverty and disables girls to be independent and self-sufficient. I think that raising awareness for this cause and funding their education will definitely help many girls and make our world a better place!

We are very determined to make things better for girls living in bad educational situations in developing countries in South Asia, Latin America, and Africa. We chose to work together to create something that is bound to lead to reform.
